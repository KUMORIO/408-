>The Winter Solstice marks the shortest daylight and longest night of the year, signaling the onset of the coldest season. Following the solstice, temperatures gradually drop, leading to reduced outdoor activities. Farmers engage less in field work, focusing on maintaining irrigation systems, protecting crops from freezing, and preparing for spring planting. Chinese tradition places great importance on the Winter Solstice, celebrated as a festival in many regions with diverse customs. In the northern regions, there's a tradition of eating dumplings (jiaozi) on this day, while in the south, it's customary to consume sweet glutinous rice balls known as tangyuan.
