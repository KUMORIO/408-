## 2019 - 学术活动组织信函

 **Directions: Suppose Professor Smith asked you to plan a debate on the theme of city traffic. **

**Write him an email to **

**1) suggest a specific topic with your reasons, and **

**2) tell him about your arrangements. **

**You should write about 100 words on the ANSWER SHEET. Do not use your own name. Use “Li Ming" instead. (10 points)**

>Dear Professor Smith,
>
>I hope this message finds you well. As per your request, I have given considerable thought to planning the upcoming debate on the theme of city traffic. After careful consideration, I would like to suggest the topic "Should Private Cars Be Restricted in City Centers?" for our discussion.
>
>This topic is particularly relevant due to the increasing congestion in urban areas, which leads to environmental pollution, time wastage, and public health concerns. By debating this issue, we can encourage critical thinking and foster a greater understanding of the complex challenges associated with urban transportation.
>
>As for the arrangements, the debate will be held next Friday at 3 PM in the main **auditorium**. We have already **secured** two eloquent speakers from the urban planning department and the environmental science faculty to represent each side of the argument. Additionally, I have organized a **panel** of judges consisting of respected professors from our university to ensure fairness and impartiality during the event.
>
>Please let me know if you have any suggestions or concerns regarding the proposed topic and arrangements. I look forward to your valuable feedback and guidance.
>
>Best regards,
>
>Li Ming

## 2020 -通知地点，给予提示

**Suppose you are planning a tour of a historical site for a group of international students. Write them an email to **

**1) tell them about the site, and **

**2) give them some tips for the tour. **

**You should write about 100 words on the ANSWER SHEET. Do not use your own name. Use "Li Ming" instead. (10 points)**

>Dear International Students,
>
>Greetings! I am delighted to announce our upcoming visit to one of the city's most treasured historical landmarks, the Ancient City Walls, on Saturday, March 25th. This iconic structure dates back over 600 years, offering a glimpse into the rich history and architectural heritage of our region. The walls, originally built during the Ming Dynasty, stand as a testament to the ingenuity and craftsmanship of our ancestors.
>
>To make the most of your experience, here are a few tips:
>
>1. Wear comfortable shoes as we will be walking quite a bit.
>2. Bring sunscreen and a hat if the weather forecast predicts sun.
>3. Carry a water bottle to stay hydrated throughout the tour.
>4. Prepare questions or topics you're curious about; our guide is eager to share insights.
>5. Don't forget your camera to capture memories.
>
>We look forward to exploring this historical treasure with you. Please let us know if you have any questions or special requirements.
>
>Best wishes, Li Ming

>亲爱的国际学生们，
>
>问好！我很高兴地宣布，我们将于3月25日（周六）参观本市最珍贵的历史地标之一——古城墙。这座标志性的建筑拥有超过600年的历史，让我们得以一窥本地区丰富的历史和建筑遗产。城墙最初建于明朝，见证了先人的智慧和工艺，至今仍屹立不倒。
>
>为了让您的体验更加充实，以下是一些小贴士：
>
>1. 穿着舒适的鞋子，因为我们将步行相当长的距离。
>2. 如果天气预报显示会有阳光，请带上防晒霜和帽子。
>3. 携带水瓶，以保持整个游览过程中的水分补充。
>4. 准备一些问题或感兴趣的话题；我们的导游乐于分享深入的见解。
>5. 别忘了带上相机，记录下这段旅程的美好记忆。
>
>我们期待与您一同探索这份历史宝藏。如果您有任何问题或特殊需求，请告知我们。
>
>最美好的祝愿， 李明
>
>1. **Ancient City Walls**: 古城墙，历史遗留下来的防御工事，常用于保护古代城市。
>2. **Ming Dynasty**: 明朝，中国历史上一个重要的朝代，公元1368年至1644年。
>3. **Historical Landmarks**: 历史地标，具有重要历史意义的建筑物或地点。
>4. **Architectural Heritage**: 建筑遗产，指历史上遗留下来的建筑风格和结构。
>5. **Craftsmanship**: 工艺，特指手工制作的艺术和技巧。
>6. **Comfortable Shoes**: 舒适的鞋子，适合长时间行走或站立的鞋类。
>7. **Sunscreen**: 防晒霜，用于防止紫外线伤害皮肤的产品。
>8. **Hydrated**: 补充水分，保持身体水分充足的状态。
>9. **Insights**: 洞察，深入理解或独到见解。
>10. **Special Requirements**: 特殊要求，指个人因健康或其他原因需要的额外关注或安排。
>

## 2021-邀请参加会议

**Directions: Suppose you are organizing an online meeting.** 

**Write an email to Jack, an international student, to** 

1) **invite him to participate, and** 
2) **tell him about the details.** 

**You should write about 100 words on the ANSWER SHEET. Do not use your own name. Use "Li Ming" instead. (10 points)**

>Subject: Invitation to Participate in Online Meeting
>
>Dear Jack,
>
>I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to extend a warm invitation for you to join us at an upcoming online meeting we are organizing. As an international student, your insights and perspectives would be invaluable to our discussion.
>
>The meeting will take place on [insert date and time] via Zoom. The main purpose of the gathering is to discuss ways to enhance the academic and social experiences of international students at our university. We believe that your participation can contribute significantly to this goal.
>
>Please find attached the agenda and Zoom link for the meeting. If you have any questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to reach out to me. We look forward to your participation and hope to see you there.
>
>Best regards,
>Li Ming

>主题：邀请参加线上会议
>
>亲爱的杰克，
>
>希望这封邮件在你一切都好时到达。我写信是想热情地邀请你参加我们即将组织的一次线上会议。作为一名国际学生，你的见解和观点对我们讨论将非常宝贵。
>
>会议将于【插入日期和时间】通过Zoom平台举行。此次集会的主要目的是探讨如何提升我们大学国际学生的学术和社会体验。我们认为，你的参与将对实现这一目标做出重大贡献。
>
>请查收附件中的会议议程和Zoom链接。如果你有任何疑问或顾虑，请随时与我联系。我们期待着你的参与，希望能在线上会议中见到你。
>
>最好的祝愿，
>李明
>
>重点词汇：
>1. 延伸（extend）：表示邀请的正式表达。
>2. 国际学生（international student）：指在国外学习的学生。
>3. 见解（insights）：指深入理解或洞察。
>4. 贡献（contribute）：表示某人或某事对某一目标或结果有帮助。
>5. 议程（agenda）：指会议计划或要讨论的事项列表。
>6. Zoom链接（Zoom link）：加入Zoom会议的网络地址。
>7. 疑问（questions）：指需要解答的问题。
>8. 顾虑（concerns）：指担忧或疑虑。
>9. 参与（participation）：指参加会议、活动等的行为。
>10. 见面（see）：此处指在视频会议中看到对方。
>

## 2022 -介绍节日与邀请参加

**Directions: Suppose you are planning a campus food festival. Write an email to the international students in your university to** 

 1) **introduce the food festival, and** 

 2) **invite them to participate.** 

 **You should write about 100 words on the ANSWER SHEET. Do not use your own name. Use "Li Ming" instead. (10 points)**

**booth  cuisines** 

>Dear international students,
>	I hope this email finds you well. I am writing to introduce to you our upcoming campus food festival, which is scheduled to take place next month. This event is an opportunity for us to showcase the diverse and delicious cuisines from around the world.
>	We would like to invite you to participate in this event by showcasing your traditional food and sharing your culture with our students. You can set up a booth and cook your traditional dishes, or simply bring them to share with everyone.
>	This is a great chance for you to promote your culture and make new friends. We are looking forward to your participation and hope to see you there.
>
>Best regards,
>Li Ming

>亲爱的国际学生们，
>希望这封邮件能让你们安好。我写这封邮件是为了向你们介绍我们即将在下个月举行的校园美食节。这个活动是我们展示来自世界各地的多元和美味佳肴的一次机会。
>我们诚挚地邀请你们参与这次活动，通过展示你们的传统美食，与我们的学生们分享你们的文化。你们可以设立一个摊位烹饪你们的传统菜肴，或者直接带来与大家分享。
>这是一个宣传你们文化、结交新朋友的好机会。我们期待你们的参与，希望在那里见到你们。
>此致敬礼，
>李明
>
>1. **diverse** (adj.) - 意味着种类繁多，不同。在这个上下文中，指的是来自世界各地的不同美食。
>2. **cuisines** (n.) - 特指不同地区或国家的烹饪风格和食物类型。
>3. **scheduled** (adj.) - 表示计划好或安排好的。这里提到的是校园美食节的时间安排。
>4. **opportunity** (n.) - 机会，指的是可以做某事的时机或情境。
>5. **showcase** (v.) - 展示或展出。在此邮件中，意味着展示传统食物和文化。
>6. **traditional** (adj.) - 传统的，指的是世代相传的习俗、做法或食品。
>7. **booth** (n.) - 小屋，摊位。在这里指的是活动现场设立的供参与者展示食物的小型空间。
>8. **promote** (v.) - 推广或宣传。邮件中提到的推广文化，意为通过分享食物来传播和介绍各自的文化。
>9. **participation** (n.) - 参与，指的是个人或团体在活动中的加入和贡献。
>10. **looking forward to** (phrase) - 期待，表示对未来的某事充满期待。

>
