**Directions:**
**Suppose you are going to study abroad and share an apartment with John, a local student. Write him an email to**
**1)**
**tell him about your living habits, and**
**2)**
**ask for advice about living there.**
**You should write about 100 words on the ANSWER SHEET.**
**Do not use your own name. Use “Li Ming" instead.**
**Do not write your address. (10 points)**

>Subject: Looking forward to sharing an apartment with you - Li Ming
>
>Dear John,
>
>I hope this email finds you well. I am Li Ming, your future flatmate from China, excited to start my studies at our university soon. As we'll be sharing a living space, I thought it would be beneficial to introduce my lifestyle habits so that we can create a comfortable environment for both of us.
>
>Firstly, I am quite tidy and organized, preferring to keep common areas clean and clutter-free. I enjoy cooking and often prepare meals in the evening, but I'm mindful of noise and always clean up afterward. I am also respectful of personal space and quiet times, aiming to maintain a peaceful home atmosphere.
>
>As for your advice, I'd greatly appreciate any tips on local customs or must-know information about living in [City/Region]. Are there any particular house rules or practices you follow that I should be aware of? Any insights into good grocery stores, transport options, or places to explore would also be very helpful.
>
>Looking forward to meeting you and starting this new chapter together.
>
>Best regards,
>Li Ming

## 2020 -通知地点，给予提示

**Suppose you are planning a tour of a historical site for a group of international students. Write them an email to **

**1) tell them about the site, and **

**2) give them some tips for the tour. **

**You should write about 100 words on the ANSWER SHEET. Do not use your own name. Use "Li Ming" instead. (10 points)**

>Dear International Students,
>
>Greetings! I am delighted to announce our upcoming visit to one of the city's most treasured historical landmarks, the Ancient City Walls, on Saturday, March 25th. This iconic structure dates back over 600 years, offering a glimpse into the rich history and architectural heritage of our region. The walls, originally built during the Ming Dynasty, stand as a testament to the ingenuity and craftsmanship of our ancestors.
>
>To make the most of your experience, here are a few tips:
>
>1. Wear comfortable shoes as we will be walking quite a bit.
>2. Bring sunscreen and a hat if the weather forecast predicts sun.
>3. Carry a water bottle to stay hydrated throughout the tour.
>4. Prepare questions or topics you're curious about; our guide is eager to share insights.
>5. Don't forget your camera to capture memories.
>
>We look forward to exploring this historical treasure with you. Please let us know if you have any questions or special requirements.
>
>Best wishes, Li Ming

>亲爱的国际学生们，
>
>问好！我很高兴地宣布，我们将于3月25日（周六）参观本市最珍贵的历史地标之一——古城墙。这座标志性的建筑拥有超过600年的历史，让我们得以一窥本地区丰富的历史和建筑遗产。城墙最初建于明朝，见证了先人的智慧和工艺，至今仍屹立不倒。
>
>为了让您的体验更加充实，以下是一些小贴士：
>
>1. 穿着舒适的鞋子，因为我们将步行相当长的距离。
>2. 如果天气预报显示会有阳光，请带上防晒霜和帽子。
>3. 携带水瓶，以保持整个游览过程中的水分补充。
>4. 准备一些问题或感兴趣的话题；我们的导游乐于分享深入的见解。
>5. 别忘了带上相机，记录下这段旅程的美好记忆。
>
>我们期待与您一同探索这份历史宝藏。如果您有任何问题或特殊需求，请告知我们。
